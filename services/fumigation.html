Our Method

The PHS Fumigation Process

Site Assessment & Pest Survey

Technical team inspects infestation levels, commodity quantities, structural conditions, and sealing requirements before any fumigation begins.

Sealing & Preparation

Facility sealed with heavy-duty tarpaulins or available structural sealing — all vents, gaps, and entry points sealed to ensure gas retention.

Fumigant Application

WHO-approved phosphine fumigants applied at scientifically calculated dosage rates by certified team in full PPE. Access restricted to authorized personnel only.

Exposure Period & Gas Monitoring

Continuous gas concentration monitoring throughout the exposure period (typically 3–5 days depending on commodity and ambient temperature) to confirm lethal concentrations maintained.

Aeration & Clearance

Controlled aeration until phosphine levels reach safe limits (<0.1 ppm). Official clearance issued by PHS technical supervisor before re-entry permitted.

Post-Fumigation Report & Certificate

Full fumigation certificate with gas concentrations, dates, dosage rates, and technician credentials — suitable for export documentation and regulatory compliance.
