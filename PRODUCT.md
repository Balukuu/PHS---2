# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Facilities managers, operations directors, and procurement officers at Kampala's high-end hotels, embassies, NGOs, international schools, warehouses, and export houses; plus affluent homeowners. They are evaluating a safety-critical vendor: they need proof of certification, discretion, and professionalism before they call. Secondary audience: international partners (WHO programs, Syngenta) verifying credibility.

## Product Purpose

Marketing website for Public Health Solutions (U) Ltd. (phs-africa.com), Uganda's premium certified pest management company (est. 2010, subsidiary of Pangolin International). The site's job is to make PHS look like the obvious choice for institutions that cannot afford pest problems or chemical-safety scandals, and to convert visits into quote requests (form, phone, WhatsApp).

## Brand Personality

Assured, exacting, established. The voice of an institution: an embassy brass plaque, an embossed certificate, a field manual with gold-foil stamping. Calm authority over salesmanship. Never alarmist, never discount-flyer pest control.

## Anti-references

- Local competitor sites: clip-art bugs, red "KILL PESTS" urgency, WhatsApp-green chaos, banned-chemical vibes.
- Generic SaaS landing-page template: icon-card grids, gradient text, hero-metric blocks.
- Fashion-editorial affectation: this is an operational, safety-critical service, not a magazine.

## Design Principles

1. Certification is the product: credentials (WHO, EU, US EPA) are presented as quiet fact, not badges shouting for attention.
2. Discretion reads as luxury: restraint, generous space, hairline rules; gold is an accent, never wallpaper.
3. Show the standard: real photography of clean environments and professional teams beats illustration.
4. One idea per fold: deliberate pacing, no crowding.
5. Every detail calibrated: like the Hudson pumps in the copy, the site itself must feel precisely maintained.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA target. Visible focus states, sufficient contrast on green/gold combinations, reduced-motion support, semantic landmarks. Audience includes low-bandwidth mobile users in Uganda: keep payloads light, lazy-load imagery.
