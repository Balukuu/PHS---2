## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment:** Better than slop, but the lineage shows. The literal traps were avoided well: the stats area is a clean card (not a gradient hero-metric block), the six features live in one unified divided panel (not identical floating cards), the hero inset is a solid card (not glass), and there are no gradient-text or side-stripe borders. The remaining tell is the **two-tone muted-continuation heading used as section grammar** on four sections (Intro, Offer, Solutions, Cases). Repeated, it reads as a template signature rather than a voice. Copy is clean of em dashes.

**Deterministic scan:** Bundled detector engine not found (`detect.mjs` ran but reported "bundled detector not found"), so the automated scan is unavailable. Substituted a manual pass against the impeccable bans and the ui-ux-pro-max Quick Reference. Manual findings: tab control does not update `aria-selected` on switch; several micro-labels fall below the 4.5:1 small-text contrast bar; one false affordance (hero inset arrow).

**Visual overlays:** Not available. No browser automation in this session, so no `[Human]` overlay tab. Assessment B independence: degraded (sequential, detector engine + browser both unavailable).

## Overall Impression

The redesign achieves what was asked: light, rounded, friendly, cohesive at the component level (pills, soft cards, green chips, rounded hero cards). The home page has a clear emotional arc and a strong end (CTA band). The single biggest opportunity is **finishing the system**: the inner pages adopted the new shell (nav, hero cards, buttons) but kept the old section-heading grammar, so the site still reads as two design languages once you scroll past any hero. Second: the softening traded away some of the institutional "proof first" authority the audience (procurement/facilities) actually buys on.

## What's Working

- **Cohesive rounded shell.** Pills, 16px radii, soft neutral shadows, and green icon chips are applied consistently; the rounded inset hero card and the dark rounded CTA band clearly belong to one family.
- **Feature panel as a unified surface.** Six differentiators in one divided panel is the right call; it sidesteps the identical-card-grid cliché while staying scannable.
- **Emotional arc + strong close.** Hero competence to certification reassurance to social proof to a confident CTA with two trust checks. Peak-end is handled.

## Priority Issues

- **[P1] Half-applied design system (home vs inner pages).** Inner-page section headings still use `.section-label` + `.section-divider` + deep-green `<h2>`, while home uses two-tone `.h-lead`. Below the hero, the site looks like two products. *Why it matters:* consistency is the whole point of "use the same system across the site"; the seam is visible on every inner page. *Fix:* port the two-tone heading + the home section rhythm to inner pages, or retire two-tone and unify on one heading style everywhere. *Command:* `layout`.

- **[P1] Accessibility gaps in new components.** (a) The case-study tabs toggle a `.active` class but never update `aria-selected`, so screen readers always announce tab 1 as selected. (b) Micro-labels below 4.5:1: `cert-circle__sub` (~9px `#717771` on white), and `home-cta__title .muted` at `rgba(255,255,255,0.42)` on dark green. *Why it matters:* Sam (screen-reader / low-vision) gets wrong state and unreadable labels; this is the CRITICAL category. *Fix:* sync `aria-selected`/`tabindex` in the tab handler; raise muted text to a token that clears 4.5:1 (or enlarge the cert sub-label). *Command:* `harden`.

- **[P2] Two-tone heading is now section grammar.** The muted-continuation `.h-lead` appears on four sections; repetition is an AI-scaffolding tell. *Why it matters:* undercuts the "distinctive, not template" goal you chose. *Fix:* vary heading treatment per section (one bold statement, one kicker+heading, one oversize numeral, etc.); reserve two-tone for one hero-adjacent moment. *Command:* `typeset`.

- **[P2] Hero inset is a false affordance, and the hero lacks a proof line.** The inset "Spaces we protect" card has an arrow-in-circle chip implying it links somewhere, but it is a static `<figure>`. Separately, the hero dropped the old supporting sentence naming the audience (hotels, embassies, warehouses, schools), which is exactly the reassurance a first-time evaluator scans for. *Why it matters:* Jordan taps the arrow and nothing happens; Patricia can't tell in 5 seconds who this is for. *Fix:* make the inset a real link (to services/about) or remove the arrow; add one supporting line under the H1. *Command:* `clarify`.

- **[P2] Stats strip is inconsistent and partly unverifiable.** Two animated numbers (15+, 60%) sit beside a text slot ("WHO · EU · EPA"), breaking the parallel; the "60% of the high-end hospitality market" is a soft, unsourced claim a procurement officer will discount; Poppins (non-tabular) digits jitter the card width during count-up. *Why it matters:* to the buying audience, a shaky stat reads as marketing fluff and weakens credibility. *Fix:* make all three the same shape (or drop to two solid numbers + one credential line styled as a label, not a stat), soften/qualify the 60% claim, and use `font-variant-numeric: tabular-nums` on counters. *Command:* `clarify` / `polish`.

## Persona Red Flags

**Patricia (Facilities/Procurement Manager — project persona, from PRODUCT.md):** Buys on proof and discretion. The new hero leads with aspiration (resort, "higher standard") and pushes the credentials into small circular chips with ~9px labels below the fold. The unsourced "60%" reads as a flyer claim, not institutional fact. Risk: the softer look reads as "consumer pest spray," not "embassy-grade vendor."

**Jordan (First-Timer):** Hero inset arrow chip looks tappable but does nothing. Case-study tabs sit *below* their content, which is backwards from the usual tabs-on-top convention, so the relationship isn't obvious at a glance.

**Sam (Accessibility):** Tab `aria-selected` never changes, so the active sector is misannounced. `cert-circle__sub` and the CTA muted clause fall under 4.5:1. No current-page indicator in the nav, so location is unclear without sight.

**Casey (Mobile):** Mostly good (hero inset hidden, stats stack, pills go full-width). But the case-study tab pills are ~36-40px tall, under the 44px touch-target minimum, and sit close together.

## Minor Observations

- No active/current-page state in the top nav on any page (`nav-state-active`).
- Top nav carries 7 items plus two dropdowns; the Services mega-menu is ~20 links (high momentary cognitive load). Pre-existing, not from this redesign.
- Full-bleed `.industry-section` split image bands on industries.html are not rounded like the rest of the system.
- Dead CSS remains for retired home components (`pest-grid`, `stats-section`, `split-section`, `testimonials-slider`).
- PRODUCT.md/DESIGN.md now contradict the shipped design (still describe gold/cream + institutional anti-SaaS).

## Questions to Consider

- What would a "proof-first" version of this hero look like, that keeps the friendly rounding but leads with certification the way Patricia buys?
- If the two-tone heading appeared exactly once on the page instead of four times, where would it earn the most?
- Should the inner pages rise to the home's grammar, or should the home come down a notch to meet them, whichever is faster to make fully consistent?
